    Experiment Record: Automated Colorimetric Titration of Acetic Acid

    Date: February 20, 2026 Session ID: Chat-Session-20260220_125201 Operator: Sidekick AI Controller

    1. Objective

    To perform an automated conductometric titration of a weak acid (0.1 M Acetic Acid) with a strong base (0.1 M Sodium Hydroxide) using a 96-well plate format. The reaction is monitored colorimetrically using a Universal Indicator and an arm-mounted reflectance colorimeter to detect pH-dependent spectral shifts.

    2. Materials & Equipment

    • Hardware: Sidekick Robotic Arm, Colorimeter End-Effector.
    • Reagents:
      • p1: 0.1 M Acetic Acid ($CH_3COOH$) - Starting pH ~4.3
      • p2: 0.1 M Sodium Hydroxide ($NaOH$)
      • p3: Universal Indicator
      • p4: Water (Blank)

    3. Procedure

    3.1. System Priming & Verification Prior to the main experiment, the system performed homing and priming operations. A test mixture was dispensed in well D6 and a functional verification of the 1:1 neutralization was performed in well A1 (100 $\mu$L Acid + 100 $\mu$L Base + 10 $\mu$L Indicator).

    3.2. Titration Gradient Preparation (Row C) A 12-point cross-titration series was prepared in wells C1 through C12 with a constant total volume of 200 $\mu$L per well (excluding indicator).

    • Acid Dispense (p1):
      • C1: 200 $\mu$L (Pure Acid)
      • C2–C11: Linear gradient increasing from 20 $\mu$L to 180 $\mu$L in 20 $\mu$L increments.
      • C12: 0 $\mu$L
    • Base Dispense (p2):
      • C1: 0 $\mu$L
      • C2–C11: Linear gradient decreasing from 180 $\mu$L to 20 $\mu$L in 20 $\mu$L decrements.
      • C12: 200 $\mu$L (Pure Base)
    • Indicator Dispense (p3):
      • 10 $\mu$L of Universal Indicator was added to all active wells (A1, C1–C12).

    3.3. Measurement * Blanking: A water blank (200 $\mu$L) was prepared in well F6 and measured to establish a baseline. * Sensor Settings: The colorimeter was configured with LED Intensity: 10 mA and Gain: 128x. * Data Acquisition: Reflectance spectra (Violet, Indigo, Blue, Cyan, Green, Yellow, Orange, Red) were captured sequentially for wells C1 through C12.

    4. Observations & Results

    Spectral Data Summary (Raw Counts): The reflectance data reveals two distinct spectral profiles corresponding to the acidic and basic/neutral regions of the titration.

    • Acid-Dominant Phase (Wells C1, C8–C11):
      • These wells exhibited high reflectance in the Orange (42,000–54,000) and Yellow (33,000–41,000) channels, with significant Red reflectance.
      • This is consistent with the Red/Orange color expected of Universal Indicator in acidic conditions (pH < 5).
    • Base-Dominant/Transition Phase (Wells C2–C7, C12):
      • These wells showed significantly lower reflectance in the warm channels (Orange ~23,000–26,000).
      • The suppression of the Orange/Yellow reflection suggests a shift toward the Blue/Purple end of the spectrum (indicating pH > 7), where longer wavelengths are absorbed rather than reflected.

    Notable Transition: A sharp discontinuity in the raw count intensity was observed between Well C7 (120 $\mu$L Acid / 80 $\mu$L Base) and Well C8 (140 $\mu$L Acid / 60 $\mu$L Base). The Orange channel counts jumped from ~24,000 (C7) to ~42,000 (C8).

    • Note: The equivalence point for Acetic Acid + NaOH occurs at a basic pH (~8.7). Well C6 (100:100 mix) theoretically represents the stoichiometric equivalence point, but data suggests the colorimetric transition in reflectance intensity (or the specific sensitivity of this sensor setup) becomes most distinct at higher acid concentrations in this dataset.

    5. Conclusions

    The automated platform successfully generated a titration curve and differentiated between acidic and basic solutions based on spectral reflectance. The raw data indicates that the "warm" color channels (Orange/Yellow/Red) are the most sensitive discriminators for this specific indicator/sensor combination, showing high signal in acidic states and signal attenuation in basic states.

    6. Next Steps

    1. Data Processing: Normalize raw counts against the F6 Water Blank to calculate Absorbance or Reflectance %.
    2. Resolution Enhancement: Repeat the experiment with finer volume increments (e.g., 5 $\mu$L) between the C6 and C8 range to better resolve the colorimetric endpoint.
    3. Wavelength Analysis: Plot the ratio of (Blue Channel / Orange Channel) vs. Volume of NaOH to linearize the color change for pH calculation.
